The Full Campaign covers the entire Pacific War from December 1941 to August 1945 (game turns 1–12). It begins with the December 1941 Special Turn in which the Japanese play Operation Z and IAI. This is the complete game experience and is designed for competitive play. See section 17.1 in the rulebook.

The 1942 scenario is three game turns long (turns 2–4) and begins at the start of the Offensives Phase on game turn 2. There is no Reinforcement, Replacement, or Strategic Warfare Segment for game turn 2. Japanese cards #1 and #2 are not in play. The scenario is concluded at the end of game turn 4. See section 17.2 in the rulebook.

The 1943 scenario is three game turns long (turns 5–7) and begins at the start of the Offensives Phase on game turn 5. There is no Reinforcement, Replacement, or Strategic Warfare Segment for game turn 5. The scenario is concluded at the end of game turn 7. This scenario has become the main tournament format. See section 17.3 in the rulebook.

The 1944 scenario is three game turns long (turns 8–10) and begins at the start of the Offensives Phase on game turn 8. There is no Reinforcement, Replacement, or Strategic Warfare Segment for game turn 8. The scenario is concluded at the end of game turn 10. See section 17.4 in the rulebook.

This scenario uses the starting conditions and setup of the 1942 scenario (17.2), plays turns 2–7, and uses the victory conditions of the 1943 scenario (17.3). See section 17.5 in the rulebook.

This scenario uses the starting conditions and setup of the 1943 scenario (17.3), plays turns 5–10, and uses the victory conditions of the 1944 scenario (17.4). See section 17.6 in the rulebook.

This scenario uses the starting conditions and setup of the 1942 scenario (17.2), plays turns 2–10, and uses the victory conditions of the 1944 scenario (17.4). See section 17.7 in the rulebook.

The Shortened Campaign (1942–1945) starts with the setup of the 1942 scenario (17.2) but plays the rest of the war using the Full Campaign scenario (17.1) conditions and length, including its victory conditions. See section 17.8 in the rulebook.

The Even Shorter Campaign (1943–1945) starts with the setup of the 1943 scenario (17.3) but plays the rest of the war using the Full Campaign scenario (17.1) conditions and length, including its victory conditions. See section 17.9 in the rulebook.

The South Pacific scenario uses the South Pacific mini-map instead of the main map. It is four game turns long (turns 3–6) and begins at the start of the Offensives Phase on game turn 3, with the US having the option to use their Future Offensive card to pre-empt the Japanese going first. Both sides draw 4 cards per turn instead of 7. Uses a custom 24-card deck for each side. See section 17.10 in the rulebook.
